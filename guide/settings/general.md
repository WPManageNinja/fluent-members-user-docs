# Settings — Overview

Fluent Members has five settings screens. This page tells you what lives where, so you never have to hunt.

**Here's what you'll learn:**
- What each settings tab does
- Which settings are "set and forget" vs ones you'll revisit
- Where to click first when you need to customise a specific behaviour

**Before we start:** You don't need any prior setup. Settings tabs are safe to explore — they only take effect when you save.

---

## The five settings tabs

Go to **Fluent Members → Settings**. You'll see five tabs across the top:

| Tab | What it controls | Visit it when… |
|---|---|---|
| **Public Contents** | The allow-list of pages that always stay public | You're enabling site-wide protection |
| **Login Popup** | The popup that appears for non-logged-in visitors on protected content | You want a login box instead of a redirect |
| **Mailing** | The "From" name/email, header logo, and footer for every notification email | Right after installing, before going live |
| **Email Notifications** | The content of individual emails (like the Welcome email) | You want to customise what members receive |
| **Partial Content** | The default teaser length, overlay message, and CTA styling | You're using the teaser-paywall pattern |

Each tab is its own page:

- [Public Contents](./public-contents.md)
- [Login Popup](./login-popup.md)
- [Mailing](./mailing.md)

## "Set and forget" vs revisit

### Set once, forget
- **Mailing** — set your From name, email, logo, footer once at launch. Rarely needs changes.
- **Login Popup** — configure colours and copy once to match your brand.
- **Partial Content** — once the overlay looks right, leave it alone.

### Revisit as your site grows
- **Public Contents** — whenever you add a new essential page (privacy, new landing page, etc.)
- **Email Notifications** — iterate on your welcome email copy over time
